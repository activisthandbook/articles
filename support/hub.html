This is a guide for Rebel Hub coordinators. In this article, you will learn what Rebel Hubs are and how to start one.

In this guide, we assume you have already signed up as hub coordinator. If you haven't done so yet, apply as hub coordination.

What is a Rebel Hub?

A Rebel Hub is a local community of activists and rebel trainers. They are part of our international network of Rebel Hubs.

A Rebel Hub trains change-makers and collects the knowledge and experiences of activists in their city or country. They support existing movements and help organise campaigns.

With our train-the-trainer programme, you will learn how to train activists from experienced campaigners around the globe.

Start a Rebel Hub

In this section, you will learn how to set up a Rebel Hub in your city or country.

1. Follow train-the-trainers programme

Most importantly: know that you are not alone in this. Experienced activists from around the globe will help you get started. We have an extensive training programme for people who want to start a Rebel Hub.

Our idea is simple: instead of training activists directly, we train trainers. This way, we create an accelerating chain reaction and make maximum impact. By following our academy, you will learn:

  • How to build a local community of activists and trainers
  • How to organise and facilitate a 2-day writing event as part of the International Rebel Assembly, to collect the experiences and knowledge of activists in your community
  • How to grow & strengthen your hub so you can make long-lasting impact
  • How to set up a training programme and support activist campaigns.

2. Build a local community

Set up communication channels:

  • Create a WhatsApp group chat
  • Create social media accounts on Facebook and Instagram.

Start your outreach campaign:

  • Reach out to friends
  • Reach out to local activists movements

Recruit volunteers:

  • Set up roles and share vacancies. For example, set up roles to define who will do social media, outreach, logistics, 
  • Welcome and train new people
  • Organise community building events to get to know each other. For example, join each other for dinner, go for a hike and get to know each other better by discussing different working styles.

3. Join the International Rebel Assembly

In July, we are organising an International Rebel Assembly. During this 2-day event, activists will be coming together in local Rebel Hubs across the globe. Together, we will be writing guides for activists. 

  • Choose themes you want to write about.
  • Recruit participants.
  • Find a location to host your Rebel Hub. For example, get together in someones living room, at university, the library or community centre. If you cannot find a location for free, you might have to rent a space.
