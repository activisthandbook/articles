Below we have collected a list of platforms where you can find photos and videos of protestors for free. Many of these have been published under a creative commons licence, or similar.

Make sure to double check the licence for all material you use. And even if no attribution is required, usually, authors appreciate a little thank you. 

Protest pictures 

  • Pexels: More than 3000 free stock photos and videos. No attribution is required under their licence.
  • Unsplash: Over 2 million free stock photos. No attribution is required under their licence.
  • Pixabay
  • 350 Flickr: Over 69,000 photos of climate activists. The licence differs per photo.
  • Fridays for Future Germany (licence unclear): Photos of Fridays for Future protests in Germany.

Activist video footage

  • Coverr
  • Mazwai
  • Life of Vids: Not a very large library, but more creative looking image.
  • 350 Media Library (account required): Videos of climate activists. Their b-roll footage from 350.org projects is available to aligned filmmakers and news organizations covering the climate movement or promoting climate action, with clear attribution and/or thanks to 350.org.

Vector illustrations

  • Open Peeps: Illustrations of people under a creative commons licence.

Evaluation criteria

When adding a media resource to the list above, please add the following information:

  • Are the photos and videos free to use and under what licence?
  • Are they stock material or do they capture authentic moments?
  • How large is the media library?
  • What is the quality of the material?
