In this guide, we will help you to get started as a creative commons editor. If you are already in this role, we encourage you to edit this guide to make it fit better with your working style. This will help both you and your successor! 

Your role

Summary: As creative commons editor, your domain is the “creative commons library”. Your main goal is to update this library with new resources that are under creative commons (CC) and public domain licenses as well as leading the effort of integrating this content on our website as articles withing chapters.

Role components: Find the exact purpose, domain and accountabilities of this role in Airtable.

Integrating external content

Activist Handbook doesn't want to reinvent the wheel. Just like Wikipedia, we collect and reference readily available content on activism, and using creative commons and public domain content saves us a lot of time on research and writing of original articles. That way our teams of writers can focus on exploring topics that lack visibility and haven't been written about before.
